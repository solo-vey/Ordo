# Office Build, Technology & Operations Plan

**Company:** Artemida Technologies GmbH  
**Brand:** AI Artemida Technologies  
**Location:** Mariahilfer Straße 123, 1060 Vienna, Austria  
**Target opening:** 2027-04-01

## 1. Facility and fit-out plan

The selected facility provides **1,850 sqm** of usable area and has a **technical readiness score of 92**.

The fit-out scope covers acoustic zoning, accessible circulation, electrical upgrades, HVAC balancing, fire-safety works, and a branded reception.

The approved renovation budget is **EUR 780,000**, with completion required by **2027-02-15**.

Furniture will use ergonomic height-adjustable desks, certified task chairs, acoustic booths, modular meeting furniture, and inclusive accessible workpoints.

## 2. Technology infrastructure

The office requires dual 10 Gbps diverse-carrier internet, a redundant core, Wi-Fi 6E, and a segmented secure lab network.

A dedicated server room is required with controlled access, environmental monitoring, resilient power, and documented operating ownership.

Production and employee data must remain within the EU/EEA.

The regional technology workstream is owned by **Martin Huber, IT Operations Lead**.

## 3. Physical security and access

The required physical-security level is **High**.

Access will use role-based badges, visitor management, CCTV at entrances, and audited privileged zones.

The implementation plan includes restricted zones, visitor handling, access logging, quarterly access review, and incident escalation.

The facility must comply with OIB Richtlinie 4 and applicable Austrian accessibility requirements.

## 4. Contractors and critical suppliers

The primary general contractor is **PORR Bau GmbH**.

The programme has **6 critical suppliers** covering fit-out, furniture, network carriers, access control, life safety, and workplace services.

A supplier dependency register will track delivery dates, cost exposure, alternates, owners, and escalation thresholds.

All critical suppliers are approved with documented alternates; replacement is not currently required.

## 5. People and operating model

The company plans to hire **48 local employees** and relocate **12 employees**.

The relocation workstream covers visa support, arrival scheduling, employment onboarding, housing guidance, and legal-entity coordination.

People delivery is owned by **Elena Markovic, Regional People Lead**.

Technology operations are owned by **Martin Huber, IT Operations Lead**.

Workplace and facilities readiness are owned by **Sophie Gruber, Workplace & Facilities Lead**.

These owners form the operational-readiness group and resolve cross-functional dependencies before formal testing.

## 6. Delivery controls

- Fit-out completion by **2027-02-15**.
- Renovation spend held within **EUR 780,000**.
- Network and security infrastructure available before readiness testing.
- Six critical supplier dependencies reviewed weekly.
- Server-room controls validated before operational use.
- Relocation activities coordinated with HR, immigration, and onboarding owners.
- Blocking defects assigned an owner, deadline, and retest criterion.

The formal readiness test was completed on **2027-03-10** with status **passed**. Non-blocking observations are tracked through opening-condition controls.

## 7. Operational readiness summary

The delivery plan aligns the selected location, fit-out scope, infrastructure, security, supplier dependencies, people plan, and named operating owners.

Final opening approval remains subject to formal readiness testing, pilot operation, closure of mandatory conditions, and final package validation.
