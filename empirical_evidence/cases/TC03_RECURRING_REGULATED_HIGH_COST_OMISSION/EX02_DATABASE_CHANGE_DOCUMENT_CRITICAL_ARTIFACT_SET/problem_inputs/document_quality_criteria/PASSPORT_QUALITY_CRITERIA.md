# Passport Quality Evaluation Criteria

The passport must be a complete reviewer-ready analytical contract, not a summary.

Required: business meaning; source and QA/dev reference data; stable lookup identifiers; complete technical contract; trigger and operation conditions; exact old/new source paths; output mapping and value keys; comparison and display normalization; creation, no-op, blocking and deduplication rules; concrete manual QA scenarios with IDs; concrete unit/processor test requirements with IDs; traceability to Jira, manual QA, automation and unit tests; QA execution contract; out of scope.

Rules must contain concrete inputs and expected outcomes. “Handle missing data” or “add tests” is insufficient. Critical requirements must remain visible in the passport even when separate QA or traceability documents exist.

Failure cap: an outline/summary passport, or one missing concrete QA scenarios, unit/processor tests, mapping, normalization, blocking/no-op rules, or traceability cannot score above 50%.
