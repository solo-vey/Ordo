# Office Build, Technology & Operations Plan

**Company:** Northstar Analytics GmbH\
**Brand:** Northstar\
**Location:** Mogilska 35, 31-545 Kraków, Poland\
**Target opening:** 15 March 2027

## 1. Facility and fit-out plan

Northstar will establish its Kraków regional development and customer
support centre in a **1,850 m²** office at Mogilska 35. The selected
location received a **technical readiness score of 82/100** and is
suitable for the planned hybrid operating model.

The fit-out scope is classified as **medium**. Works include
reconfiguration of meeting rooms, installation of a new structured
cabling system, acoustic panels and refurbishment of the kitchen area.
The approved renovation budget is **EUR 740,000**, with fit-out
completion required by **12 February 2027**.

The office standard provides **110 workstations**, **8 meeting rooms**,
two phone-booth zones and a training room for 30 people. Standard
workstations will use height-adjustable desks, ergonomic chairs and two
27-inch monitors.

## 2. Technology infrastructure

The office requires two independent **2 Gbit/s internet connections**,
corporate **Wi-Fi 6E** and redundancy for critical network equipment.

A dedicated server room is required. Its implementation must include
controlled physical access, environmental monitoring and resilient power
for critical local infrastructure. Production customer data will not be
stored locally in the office. Employee personal data must remain within
the EU in accordance with the defined data-residency requirement.

The regional technology workstream is owned by **Marek Zieliński,
Regional IT Manager**.

## 3. Physical security and access

The required physical security level is **elevated**. Access will use
named employee cards, zone-based permissions, entry logging and
time-limited visitor passes.

Because the security level is elevated, the project must maintain a
dedicated security implementation plan covering restricted zones,
server-room access, visitor handling and access-log review.

The office must also provide barrier-free entry, lift access, an adapted
restroom and at least four accessible workstations.

## 4. Contractors and critical suppliers

The primary general contractor is **BuildCore Polska Sp. z o.o.** The
programme has **6 critical suppliers** whose delivery dates can directly
affect opening readiness.

A supplier dependency register must therefore be maintained for the
fit-out, network connectivity, access-control system, furniture,
fire-safety certification and specialist server-room works.

If a critical supplier cannot meet the agreed deadline or budget, the
supplier must be replaced and the impact on renovation scope, budget and
fit-out deadline reassessed before work continues.

## 5. People and operating model

Northstar plans to hire **65 local employees during the first year** and
relocate **20 employees from Germany, Czechia and Ukraine**.

Because relocation is required, a dedicated relocation workstream must
cover visa support, arrival scheduling, employment onboarding and
coordination with the local Polish entity.

The local people workstream is owned by **Anna Kowalska, Head of People
Poland**. Technology operations are owned by **Marek Zieliński, Regional
IT Manager**. Workplace and facilities readiness are owned by
**Katarzyna Nowak, Workplace Operations Lead**.

These three owners form the operational readiness group and are
responsible for resolving cross-functional dependencies before the
formal readiness test.

## 6. Delivery controls

The facility programme will use the following control points:

-   renovation and fit-out complete by **12 February 2027**;
-   network and security infrastructure available before the formal
    readiness test;
-   all six critical supplier dependencies reviewed weekly;
-   server-room controls validated before operational use;
-   relocation workstream coordinated with HR and local employment
    onboarding;
-   unresolved blocking defects routed back to the responsible facility,
    IT, security or people workstream.

The formal readiness test is scheduled for **22 February 2027**. Any
blocking defect identified during this test must create a remediation
action with an owner and deadline. The affected workstream must be
revalidated before the office proceeds to pilot operation.

## 7. Operational readiness summary

The current plan is operationally feasible within the approved facility
assumptions. The principal execution risks are the dependency on six
critical suppliers, completion of the server-room and redundant
connectivity, and coordination of the 20-person relocation programme.

The office may proceed to readiness testing when the fit-out,
technology, security and operating-owner workstreams have supplied
evidence of completion. Final opening approval remains outside this
document and is recorded in the Office Readiness & Opening Decision
Report.
